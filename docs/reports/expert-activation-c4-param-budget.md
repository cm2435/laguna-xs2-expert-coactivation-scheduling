# Laguna-XS.2 — MoE Expert Activation on C4 (with parameter budget)

**Model:** `poolside/Laguna-XS.2` · **Corpus:** `allenai/c4` (en) excerpt — **400 docs / 161,932 tokens** (seq_len ≤ 1024)
**Routing:** top-**8** of **256** experts/token across **39** sparse-MoE layers (layer 0 is dense).
Generated by forward-pass router introspection (hooks on every `LagunaTopKRouter`), in the framing of RADLADS distillation (arXiv:2505.03005): *measure what the sparse model actually uses before transferring it to a denser surrogate.*

---

## TL;DR

- **256/256 experts fire** at least once on C4 (100% global coverage), but each layer behaves like only **~158 effective experts** of 256 (exp-entropy of load).
- Routing is **skewed** — mean load **Gini ≈ 0.528**, busiest/quietest global expert load ratio **2.6×**.
- **33.4B** total parameters, but only **~3.0B active per token** — an **8.4%** active fraction (91.6% sparse). The routed-expert tensors are **94%** of the weights and **33%** of the per-token compute.

## 4. Implications for MoE→dense densification

- **~158 effective experts/layer** (of 256) on general text → the routed FFN behaves far denser than its 256-way capacity; a dense surrogate MLP per layer has a realistic reconstruction target (~19.4B of routed mass).
- **Gini ≈ 0.53** → fit the surrogate preferentially on high-traffic experts; low-traffic ones contribute little to C4 activations.
- **Co-activation structure (§3)** identifies experts that habitually fire together — natural groupings for block-wise densification / co-scheduling (the premise of the `expert-coactivation-scheduling` repo).
- **Deeper layers (L13–L25) are the most concentrated** (Gini up to 0.61, most dead experts) — cheapest to densify; the broad early/late layers (L1–L3, L26–L29) are the hardest.
- **C4 is general web text** → mix domains (code/math/kernels) so the surrogate isn't overfit to C4's active expert subset.
